Spacewar: Orbital Duel

Battle at the singularity: a one-on-one space duel fought in the gravity well of a black hole.

Beta testers, start here Learn to think in orbits

There are no straight lines here.

A black hole sits at the center and pulls on everything: you, your opponent, and every shot fired. Good pilots don't fly straight lines: they ride orbits, use the hole's gravity to swing around and line up shots, and treat the singularity as both the deadliest hazard and their best weapon. Learn to think in orbits and you'll win.

Controls

Steer

The ATTITUDE dial

Drag your finger around the circular wheel. Your ship turns by how far you sweep around the dial: the angle you travel, not how far your finger sits from the center.

Fire

Tap anywhere

A tap anywhere on the screen fires your laser, including right on the dial. Tap-tap-tap to keep the pressure on.

Thrust

Hold to burn

Hold the THRUST bar to fire your engine. By default you can also press and hold anywhere to thrust, and you can lay the controls out wherever your thumbs land.

Survive

Mind the hole

Your shots curve in the black hole's gravity, and so do you. Fly too close and you're gone. It takes several hits to down a ship, so keep flying.

Standing on shoulders

A modern tribute to Spacewar!, created in 1962 by Steve Russell and friends on MIT's PDP‑1, one of the first video games ever made. Sixty years on, the pull of the star still decides the duel.
